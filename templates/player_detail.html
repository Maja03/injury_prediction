Model Validity and Considerations
  • Avoiding overfitting beyond obvious patterns
    Use time-aware train/validation splits, cross-validation, and out-of-bag error; restrict model complexity (max depth, min samples per leaf), remove/leakage-proof “obvious” proxy features, and monitor generalization with backtests and permutation tests.
  • Research-backed variables explaining results
    Literature supports prior injury history, cumulative/acute workload, congested fixtures, age, and position-specific demands. We compare feature importance/SHAP attributions to these findings to validate non-obvious drivers.
  • Cost of a false positive (benching a healthy player)
    We surface a decision threshold tied to team cost: expected points lost, replacement quality, salary/transfer impact. Coaches can tune the threshold to reflect current fixture risk appetite.
  • Incorporating out-of-play events (e.g., off-field injuries)
    Capture as exogenous features and/or apply a manual override (see “Manual Risk Override”) so the next prediction accounts for the event; optionally log and learn these events for future model updates.
